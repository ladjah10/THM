Key Differences to Discuss

These are questions where your responses showed notable differences. Using these as conversation starters can help strengthen your understanding of each other's perspectives.

How important is it to plan finances together? (Your Finances)
John: Very important, all financial decisions should be made together
Sarah: Somewhat important, but we should maintain some financial independence

Discussion Guide:

  • What specific financial decisions do you believe should always be made together?
  • What does financial independence mean to each of you?
  • How might you create a system that respects both perspectives?

Reference: Chapter 6 of "The 100 Marriage" addresses financial alignment in depth.

What role should in-laws play in your marriage? (Your Family/Home Life)
John: Limited involvement, with clear boundaries
Sarah: Regular involvement and guidance from both families

Discussion Guide:

  • What specific boundaries would make you feel comfortable with in-law involvement?
  • What types of guidance do you find valuable from extended family?
  • How have your experiences with extended family shaped your perspective?

Reference: Chapter 9 of "The 100 Marriage" provides strategies for healthy extended family dynamics.

Additional Discussion Area

How do you prefer to resolve conflicts? (Your Marriage and Boundaries)
John: Address issues immediately and directly
Sarah: Take time to cool off before discussing sensitive topics

Discussion Guide:

  • What are the benefits you see in your approach to conflict resolution?
  • How might you create a conflict resolution process that honors both approaches?
  • When has the opposite approach been effective for you in the past?

Reference: Chapter 4 of "The 100 Marriage" explores effective conflict resolution strategies.
